I have recently completed a PhD in climate change ecology, where I utilised remote-sensed and ground-based GIS observations to develop and constrain the LPX Dynamic Global Vegetation Model Kelley2014a. I used LPX to explore potential future ecosystem resiliance, carbon stocks Kelley2014b, fire regimes Harrisonc, drought and hydrology Zeppel2014; Ukkola. Models like LPX provide useful tools for understanding the broad-scale impact of climate on ecosystem dynamics. However, these models, like so many other mapping techinqiues designed to assess ecosystem services (ES), lack the detail and accuracy required for conservation descisions that, by nesciccity, often need to target specific ES "hotspots". This is partially due to a lack of primary observational data of ES required to constrain these techniques, particularly in parts of the world most vulnerabile to future enviromental change. By utilising scale-dependancy of relationship between different ESs, SCALEFORS will help develope accurate ways to map these hotspots even in regions with limited observations. I would relish the opportunity to use the skills and experience I gained during my PhD to contribute to a research project that will help bridge such an important gap between ecological modelling and conservation. My work on developing and evaluating LPX and other vegetation models has involved processing a range of climate/GIS data products, including raw and processed satellite data, vector ground observation, climate observations and climate model output. To do this, I have used a range of statistical and GIS software including ArcGIS, ENVI, R and R statistical packages, Python and matlab. More detail on this can be found in the "support statment" section of this application. My PhD and subsequant post-doctural work involved a number of research collaborations. I led the design of a vegetation model benchmarking system Kelley2013 which involved liaising with remote sensing experts, botanists, statisticians and other modellers from three different institutions. This system has been widely adopted throughout the vegetation modelling community and is used by several other modelling groups Hantson2016. I was also involved in a consultancy programme preforming climate change risk analysis for the Royal Society for the Conservation of Nature (RSCN) in Jordan. Modelling work I conducted was used by RSCN to determine areas of relative ecosystem stability suitable for nature reserves. All of these collaborations involved visits to different institutions and workshops, some of which were international. I would be happy to continue this in my new role.
