Models like LPX provide useful tools for understanding the broad-scale impact of climate on ecosystem dynamics. However, these models, like so many other mapping techinqiues designed to assess ecosystem services (ES), lack the detail annd accuracy required for conservation descisions that, by nesciccity, often need to target specific ES "hotspots". This is partially due to a lack of primary observational data of ES required to constrain these techniques, particularly in parts of the world most vunrable to future enviromental change. By utilising scale-dependancy of relationship between different ESs, SCALEFORS will help develope accurate ways to map these hotspots even in regions with limited observations. I would relish the opportunity to use the skills and experience I gained during my PhD to contribute to a research project that will help bridge such an important gap between ecological modelling and conservation.
